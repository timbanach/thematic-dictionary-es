## Pronouns
> General order of pronouns: subject pronoun, indirect object pronoun, direct object pronoun, verb. However, if the verb is infinitive, imperative, or reflexive the direct/indirect object pronouns follow the verb.

### Prepositional pronouns
> As the name suggests, these follow a preposition; for example, "esto es para ti" (this is for you). For the most part, they are the same as the subject pronouns.

### Indirect object pronouns
> When le is followed by a pronoun starting with l, it becomes se; for example, "dáselo" (give it to him).

nos					pron		to/for us
os					pron		to/for you all (Spain, informal)
les					pron		to/for them; to/for you all (formal)

### Possesive pronouns
> Note that possesive pronouns are always preceded by the definite article; for example "el mío" or "la mía." Also, these are actually relative adjectives, and agree in gender and number with the noun they modify.
mío(s)/mía(s)				adj, pron	mine		
tuyo(s)/tuya(s)				adj, pron	yours (informal)
suyo(s)/suya(s)				adj, pron	his/hers; its; yours (formal)
nuestro(s)/nuestra(s)			adj, pron	ours
vuestro(s)/vuestra(s)			adj, pron	yours (Spain, informal)
suyo(s)/suya(s)				adj, pron	theirs; yours (formal)

### Relative pronouns

> In addition to being interrogative adverbs, "cuando" and "donde" can also be used as relative pronouns:
